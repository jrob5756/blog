Welcome to my corner of the internet.

I'm Jason, a Principal Software Engineer at Microsoft working in the Azure Core Office of the CTO. I spend my days incubating solutions at the intersection of AI tooling, distributed systems, and developer experience — taking ideas from "this could work" to "this is running in production."

## Why a blog?

I've been meaning to write more publicly for a while. Most of my work lives behind corporate walls — design documents, architecture reviews, prototype repos that eventually graduate to product teams. But the thinking behind that work — the patterns, the trade-offs, the lessons from failures — is universally useful.

This site is where I'll share:

- **Technical deep dives** on distributed systems, control plane architecture, and cloud-native patterns
- **AI tooling observations** from building MCP servers, developer productivity tools, and LLM-powered workflows
- **Engineering culture notes** on leading without authority, prototype-to-product transitions, and remote collaboration

## The stack

This site is built with [Hugo](https://gohugo.io/) and the [PaperMod](https://github.com/adityatelange/hugo-PaperMod) theme, deployed to [Cloudflare Pages](https://pages.cloudflare.com/). The entire thing costs about $10/year (just the domain). I chose Hugo for its speed, zero dependencies, and excellent multi-section support — important because this site also hosts a daily [news feed](/news/) synced from a separate repo.
